JobFit Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 8, 2026

JobFit ("the extension") is a Chrome extension that matches your resume against job postings found in your Gmail inbox. This policy explains what data the extension accesses, how it is used, and what is never collected.

1. Data the extension accesses

  • Gmail emails — The extension reads emails you have labeled resumes and jobposts in your Gmail account. Only those labeled emails are read; no other email is accessed.
  • Job posting pages — When a job email contains a URL, the extension may fetch the linked page to extract the full job description for analysis.

2. How your data is used

Resume text and job posting text are sent to a large language model (LLM) API of your choice (Groq, OpenAI, Anthropic, or a locally-running Ollama instance) to generate a match score and summary. No other use is made of your data.

3. Data storage

  • Analysis results are stored locally in your browser via chrome.storage.
  • Optional prompt logs are saved to your local Downloads folder for your own inspection.

4. Job history storage (opt-in)

If you choose to opt in, JobFit stores job match records for jobs scoring ≥ 85% on Cloudflare's infrastructure, linked to your Gmail address. Each record contains: job title, job URL, match score, and skill gaps. No resume text or email body content is ever stored server-side. This data is used to power future learning resource recommendations for your skill gaps.

You can enable or disable this at any time in Settings → Job History. You may request deletion of your records by contacting us via GitHub Issues.

5. Data sharing

The extension does not share your data with the developer or any third party, except for the LLM API you configure. Please review the privacy policy of your chosen LLM provider:

  • Groq Privacy Policy
  • OpenAI Privacy Policy
  • Anthropic Privacy Policy

6. Data the extension never collects

  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking of any kind.
  • No resume text or email body content is ever sent to the developer's servers.
  • No data is sold or used for advertising.

7. Permissions explained

  • identity / gmail.readonly — Required to authenticate with Gmail and read your labeled emails.
  • storage — Stores your settings and analysis results locally in the browser.
  • downloads — Saves optional prompt logs to your Downloads folder.
  • tabs / scripting / host permissions — Used to open and extract text from job posting pages linked in your emails, which may be hosted on any domain.
